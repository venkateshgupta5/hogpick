Story

16 NOV 609 Likes 29 Comments 4 share

‘For many years, I never knew where I was going’. Academics never fascinated me but extra co-curricular activities always did. I loved debating and I loved anchoring and I was always the first in my class; to raise my hand when it came to take a lead in non academic activities. Since commerce was considered easy, I joined a college in my hometown to pursue graduation. No matter, I became a commerce graduate, I never knew how to balance the accounts sheet. Since I was always good in speaking and storytelling, a lot of people convinced me to enrolL for journalism. I paid the entire fee and enrolled myself to a 9 month broadcasting communication program in a renowned media organisation but I found the environment grim and suffocating there. I left the place on the very third day.

I didn’t know what to do next so I joined a BPO in Gurgaon. It was 2003 then. For the first time, I was introduced to night life. I realised how it feels to be a nocturnal. I was going on taking calls for months and then one day I noticed that someone beside me was carefully listening to my voice. I was directed to train a batch of people the next day and all the senior trainers were sitting at the back. After the session ended, everyone appreciated and applauded me. From that day, I became a human resource trainer.

It has been 14 long years and since then I have never ever looked back again. I have trained a lot of people in various reputed multinational organisations throughout the country. I have travelled to a lot of places in the past 14 years and I have seen varied culture, diverse demographic divide and all this has fascinated me.
